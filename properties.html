Properties of Gas
Gas particles move faster and are father apart than in other forms of matter.

Definitions

  • Volume: The amount of space occupied by a gas
  • Pressure: The force on the surfaces of a container containing a gas
  • Temperature: The amount of kinetic energy of the particles in a gas

The particles/molecules of gases have more kinetic energy and potential energy than the other phases of matter. This means that particles in a gas move much faster than in other phases of matter, and are farther apart from one another.
