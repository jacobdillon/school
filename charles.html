Charles's Law
This graph shows the linear relationship between Volume and Temperature. As the volume increases, the temperature increases similarly.

Charles's law relates to the volume and temperature of a gas. The relationship between the volume and temperature of a gas is linear: as the volume of the gas increases, the temperature will increase as well, and vice versa. The constant in Charles's law is the pressure of the gas in its container. The equation for Charles's law is V1⁄T1 = V2⁄T2.
